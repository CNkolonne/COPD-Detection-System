Cough Recording Guidelines for Assistants

1

Minimize Background Noise

Ensure the room is quiet before starting.

2

Watch the Wave Pattern (the moving line) on the screen.

Only start the recording when the line is flat and steady, showing no interference from background noise.

WAVE MONITOR
Steady
3

Maintain Correct Distance

Keep a distance of 6-10 inches between the patient and the recording device (computer/microphone).

4

Capture Multiple Samples

Record exactly 3 separate cough samples from each patient.This ensures you have enough data to compare and verify the sound characteristics.

5

Monitor Patient Position

Ensure the patient is sitting upright or in a comfortable, stable position. An incorrect posture can restrict the airway and lead to an unnatural or "bad" sample

Check the Mic

Before the real recording, do a quick "mic check" to make sure the computer is picking up sound clearly.

record_voice_over
Instruction

Briefly tell the patient, "Please cough naturally into the space in front of the device," so they don't feel nervous.
